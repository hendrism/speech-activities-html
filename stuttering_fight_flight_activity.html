Fight-or-Flight & Stuttering

Learn the simple fight-or-flight steps and map your own stuttering moments to each one.

Quick Explanation

Fight-or-flight is the body’s quick alarm system for danger. It often shows up with stuttering because our brain treats speaking as risky. Here is the simple order:

FindingYou notice a possible speaking “threat.”
FreezingYour body tenses and speech muscles can lock.
FleeingYou avoid or change words to escape.
FightingYou push hard against a stuck sound.
FoldingYou give up, stay quiet, or leave.
