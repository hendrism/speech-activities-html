Communication Breakdown Practice Scenarios

For each situation, figure out what caused the communication breakdown, pick strategies to fix it, role-play the conversation, and reflect on what felt most effective.

Scenario 1: The Group Project

History Class • Small Group Presentation
You’re working with three classmates on a history presentation. You explain your research about “the trenches in France.” One teammate looks puzzled and asks, “Wait, what did you say about friends?” The others look at you, waiting.
Identify the Breakdown
What caused the misunderstanding? Check all that apply.
Sounded like another word Background noise New vocabulary Talking quickly
Choose Strategies
Pick 2–3 strategies to try.
Role-Play Ideas
Practice explaining “trenches” two different ways. One person plays you, another is the confused classmate. Try saying it once while showing a slide, and once with a slower sentence.
Reflection

Scenario 2: Coffee Order

Busy Café • Noisy Line
It’s loud at Starbucks. You order, “Can I get a large caramel latte?” The barista says, “A large… what? I didn’t catch that last part.” People are waiting behind you.
Identify the Breakdown
What caused the misunderstanding?
Background noise Fast speech Soft volume Unfamiliar order
Choose Strategies
Role-Play Ideas
One person plays the barista with background noise. Practice repeating the order with a confident voice, or showing the order typed on your phone.
Reflection

Scenario 3: The Substitute Teacher

Math Class • New Listener
There's a substitute teacher who doesn’t know you. You ask, “Can I go to my locker? I forgot my calculator.” The teacher replies, “I’m sorry, I didn’t understand you. Can you say that again?” Some classmates look over.
Identify the Breakdown
Unfamiliar listener Fast speech Quiet voice Different phrasing
Choose Strategies
Role-Play Ideas
Rehearse your request with different phrasing. One person plays the substitute and gives feedback on what was clear.
Reflection

Scenario 4: Making Weekend Plans

Phone Call • Moving from Text to Talk
You’re on the phone with a friend about weekend plans. You suggest going to see a new movie. Your friend responds, “Wait, you want to go WHERE? I couldn’t really hear you—it sounded like you said something about a movie?”
Identify the Breakdown
Phone connection Talking quickly Not enough context Background noise
Choose Strategies
Role-Play Ideas
Practice the conversation with one person covering their ears slightly to simulate a bad connection; try repeating with clearer words.
Reflection

Scenario 5: The Unfamiliar Store

Sporting Goods • Unfamiliar Listener
You’re in a sporting goods store looking for a specific brand. You ask, “Do you have any Brooks running shoes?” The employee tilts their head and says, “Do we have… I’m sorry, can you say that brand name again?”
Identify the Breakdown
Unfamiliar brand Sounds like another word Soft volume
Choose Strategies
Role-Play Ideas
Practice saying unfamiliar brand names. One person plays the employee and asks clarifying questions.
Reflection

Scenario 6: Class Discussion

English Class • Whole Group Conversation
Your teacher asks a question about a novel. You explain a character’s motivation, but the teacher says, “That’s interesting, but I’m not sure I caught which character you mean. Can you clarify?”
Identify the Breakdown
Missing names Talking quickly Soft voice
Choose Strategies
Role-Play Ideas
Practice explaining the same idea using a sentence frame: “I’m talking about [character], when they [action].”
Reflection

Scenario 7: Fast Food Drive-Through

Car Speaker • Noisy Environment
You’re ordering through a drive-through speaker. You ask for a chicken sandwich with fries. The worker replies, “Sorry, did you say chicken or beef? And did you want fries or a drink?”
Identify the Breakdown
Speaker static Multiple items Background noise
Choose Strategies
Role-Play Ideas
Use a prop (like a paper cup) as the speaker and practice ordering clearly while someone adds background noise.
Reflection

Scenario 8: The New Friend

Lunch Table • Fast Conversation
You’re excitedly telling a new friend about your favorite TV show and talking quickly. They smile but say, “Sorry, I’m having trouble following—can you slow down? I want to hear what you’re saying!”
Identify the Breakdown
Fast speech Lots of details New listener
Choose Strategies
Role-Play Ideas
Share a short story twice: once fast, once with pauses. Ask your partner which version was easier to follow.
Reflection
