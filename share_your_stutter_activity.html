Share Your Stutter

Explain your stutter in simple words, then practice saying it out loud.

Why Share?

Sharing takes away surprise and lets listeners support you. Keep it short: name it, normalize it, say what helps, and invite patience.

Name it“I stutter sometimes.”
Normalize it“It’s okay. My words just get stuck.”
Say what helps“Going slow helps me.”
Invite support“Give me a moment to finish.”

Make Your Script (Step-by-Step)

1. Pick a “loud” thing

Something that reminds you to speak up.

2. Sample script to hear

“Hey, I stutter, so you might hear me get stuck on some words. I just need a bit more time to get them out.”

3. Brainstorm: how you say you stutter
4. Brainstorm: a simple definition
5. Brainstorm: what you ask for

Draft Script

I stutter, so sounds can get stuck sometimes. Slowing down and not being interrupted helps. Please give me time and let me finish.

Write Your Final Script

How I describe my stutter

What helps me

What I want you to do

Final Script

I stutter, so sounds can get stuck sometimes. Slowing down and not being interrupted helps. Please give me time and let me finish.

Activities to Try

Share with one person

Pick a safe person (friend, family, teacher) and read your script.

Share before you speak

Before asking a question or ordering, say one line: “I stutter, so I might take a moment.”

Record yourself

Read your script aloud, record it, listen back, and notice what feels confident.

Group practice

In a small group, each person reads a script and others practice waiting and listening.

Update your ask

Rewrite your “what I want you to do” line for class, home, and online settings.
