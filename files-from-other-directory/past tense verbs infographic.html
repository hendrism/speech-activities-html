Did You Know?

Past tense verbs tell us an action already happened. While some are tricky, you're in luck! The vast majority of English verbs play by one simple rule.

This infographic breaks down how to spot them, how to use them, and what to do with the rebels that break the rules.

These are the Regular Verbs!

Most verbs are 'regular'. This is great news because they all follow one easy rule. To make a regular verb past tense, just add -d or -ed to the end.

Meet the Irregular Verbs!

Irregular verbs are the rebels. They don't follow the "-ed" rule. You have to memorize their unique forms, but they fall into a few common groups. The chart below shows the number of examples we have for each rebel crew.

Group A: The "No Change" Crew

These verbs are the exact same in the past tense!

  • Put ‚Üí He put his books away.
  • Cut ‚Üí I cut my finger.
  • Hit ‚Üí She hit the ball.
  • Set ‚Üí They set the table.

Group B: The "Vowel Switchers"

These verbs change their middle vowel for the past tense.

  • Run ‚Üí He ran a mile.
  • Sing ‚Üí We sang our songs.
  • Swim ‚Üí I swam in the lake.
  • Begin ‚Üí The movie began.

Group C: "Total Transformers"

These verbs change completely. They look nothing like their present tense form!

  • Go ‚Üí We went to the beach.
  • Eat ‚Üí I ate breakfast.
  • See ‚Üí She saw a rainbow.
  • Buy ‚Üí My mom bought groceries.
